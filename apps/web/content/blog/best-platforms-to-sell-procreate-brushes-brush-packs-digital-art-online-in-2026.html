How to Create a Procreate Brush Pack That Sells

Creating a procreate brush pack that actually sells requires more than just exporting a few brushes. You need to think about what your customers want and how they will use your brushes in their own work.

Here are the key steps we recommend:

  • Identify a niche: Focus on a specific style or use case, such as watercolor textures, comic inking, or foliage brushes. A targeted procreate brush pack sells better than a generic collection.
  • Create 5 to 10 variations: Include enough variety (line work, shading, texture, detail) so buyers feel they are getting a complete toolkit, not just a single brush.
  • Test every brush: Make sure each brush performs well across different canvas sizes and pressure settings. Quality is what drives repeat purchases.
  • Create preview images: Show the brushes in action with clear, attractive examples. Buyers want to see exactly what each brush can do before they purchase.
  • Write clear descriptions: Explain what each brush does, what Procreate version it works with, and any tips for getting the best results.

When you sell procreate brushes, your reputation as a creator depends on quality. A well-made pack leads to positive reviews, which leads to more sales.
