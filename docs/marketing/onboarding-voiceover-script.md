**Total runtime:** 6 minutes 30 seconds
**Languages needed:** English (primary) + 11 priority translations (ES, FR, DE, HI, PT, AR, UR, HE, ZH, ZH-TW, JA, KO)

This is the voice talent script. Read exactly as written. Pacing notes in [brackets]. Pronunciation hints in *italics*.

---

## Recording instructions

- **Tone:** Warm, confident, energetic. Not corporate. Imagine you're explaining to a friend why you love this product.
- **Pace:** ~150 words per minute. Don't rush. The video has room to breathe.
- **Pauses:** Use them. The script marks `[BEAT]` for 0.5-1 second pauses.
- **Emphasis:** Bold words in the script. Don't shout — just lean in slightly.
- **Numbers:** Always read out as you'd say them in conversation. "Twelve percent" not "12%". "One hundred and twenty-seven" not "127". Exception: when reading a brand or product name, read it as written (Gumroad, iLovePDF).
- **Pronunciations:**
  - "Aura" — /ˈɔːrə/ (OR-uh). Not "AW-rah".
  - "Elixio" — /iˈlɪksioʊ/ (ih-LIK-see-oh).
  - "Gumroad" — "GUM-rode".
  - "iLovePDF" — "EYE-luv-pee-dee-eff".
  - "WCAG" — "W-kag" (3 syllables).
  - "Stitch" / "Razorpay" / "Stripe" — read as written.
  - "ten percent" / "ten percent flat" — emphasize "flat" for the cost callout.

- **Format:** Record in a treated booth or quiet room. -12 LUFS integrated loudness. 48 kHz / 24-bit WAV. Mono is fine for narration. Pop filter required.
- **Take numbering:** Each scene is a separate take. Save as `vo-{scene}-{lang}.wav` (e.g. `vo-scene01-en.wav`).
- **Total runtime per take:**
  - Scene 1: 18s
  - Scene 2: 30s
  - Scene 3: 45s
  - Scene 4: 42s
  - Scene 5: 50s
  - Scene 6: 60s
  - Scene 7: 30s
  - Scene 8: 50s
  - Scene 9: 30s
  - Scene 10: 35s
  - **Total: 6 min 30 sec**

## Localization notes per language

### Spanish (es)
- "Elixio" pronounced "eh-LEEK-see-oh" in Spain, "eh-LEEK-syo" in Latin America.
- "Aura" stays the same. "Gumroad" stays the same.
- "forty-two" = "cuarenta y dos"
- "ninety percent" = "noventa por ciento"
- "ten percent flat" = "diez por ciento, sin más" (no surprises)

### French (fr)
- "Aura" stays the same.
- "Elixio" pronounced "eh-LEEK-see-oh".
- "Gumroad" stays the same.
- "Razorpay" stays the same.
- "Stripe" stays the same.
- "WCAG" — read as "double vé cé a gé" or "W-C-A-G" (4 letters).
- "WCAG two-point-one AA" = "WCAG deux-point-un double-A" (the AA rating).
- "right-to-left" = "de droite à gauche".
- Tendency: use "vous" form throughout. Don't slip into "tu".

### German (de)
- "Aura" stays the same.
- "Elixio" pronounced "eh-LIKS-ee-oh".
- Compound words are common — the script is intentionally short to keep VO natural.
- "WCAG" — read as "ve-tsa-ay-gay" or just "WCAG".
- "forty-two" = "zweiundvierzig".
- "ten percent" = "zehn Prozent".

### Hindi (hi)
- "Aura" stays the same (romanized).
- "Elixio" stays the same.

## Quality checklist

- [ ] All numbers read as words, not digits
- [ ] Brand names pronounced correctly
- [ ] Pacing is conversational, not announcer-y
- [ ] Background noise floor below -60 dB
- [ ] Peaks below -3 dB
- [ ] Loudness -12 to -16 LUFS integrated
- [ ] No clicks, pops, or mouth noises
- [ ] Final delivery: 12 files (1 per language) for each scene = 120 WAVs
